Famous Olympians

Throughout USA swimming history, there have been numerous famous Olympians, who are noted for their accomplishments in swimming. Some of them are very well-known: Michael Phelps, for example. However, there are also those who are not as well-known, such as Lily King. To be considered an Olympian, you must medal at the Olympics, whether it be gold, silver, or bronze in an individual event or a relay. Noted below are two swimmers-one male and one female-who are Olympians.


Michael Phelps



Most likely the most known Olympic swimming athlete, Michael Phelps is recognized around the world. He is known as the "King of the Olympics", and is recognized worldwide. His best events include the 100M butterfly and the 200M butterfly. He's won a total of 28 Olympic medals, the most of anyone to ever participate in the Olympics. In Athens (2004), he won 6 gold medals in the 400 IM, 200 Butterfly, 4x200 freestlye relay, 200 IM, 100 buterfly, and the 4x100 medley relay. He also won two bronze medals in the 200 freestyle and the 4x100 freestyle relay. In Beijing 2008, he won 8 gold medals: 400 IM, 4x100 free relay, 200 freestlye, 200 butterfly, 4x200 freestyle relay, 200 IM, 100 butterfly, and the 4x100 medley relay. In London 2012, he won 4 gold medals: 4x200 freestyle relay, 200 IM, 100 butterfly, and 4x100 medley relay. He also won 2 silver medals: 4x100 freestyle relay and the 200 Butterfly. Lastly, in Rio 2016, he won 5 gold medals: 4x100 freestlye relay, 200 butterfly, 4x200 freestyle relay, and the 4x100 medley relay. He also won one silver medal: 100 butterfly. Rebecca Soni


Rebecca Soni was an Olympic swimmer who specialized in the 100M breaststroke and the 200M breaststroke. She is a six-time Olympian and was the first female swimmer to go under 2 minutes and 20 seconds in the 200M breaststroke. As a 17-year-old at the 2004 U.S. Olympic Team Trials, Soni finished 15th overall in the 100-meter breaststroke and 11th overall in the 200-meter breaststroke. The following year, at the 2005 World Championship Trials, Rebecca Soni just missed a spot on the 2005 World Aquatic team after finishing third in the 200-meter breaststroke. Over the course of her career, Rebecca Soni has won a total of 6 Olympic Medals. In Bejing 2008, she won three medals: gold in the 200 breastroke, silver in the 100 breastroke, and silver again in 4x100 medley relay. In London 2012, she won another three medals: gold medal in 200 breastrstroke, anotherg gold in 4x100 medley relay, and silver in the 100 breaststroke.
